# Tone presets

Each preset shifts the language patterns Claude uses when generating slide content. Pick one in Step 7.

## explore — exploratory partnership / early conversation

**Use when:** the audience hasn't agreed to anything yet. You're testing fit, not closing.

**Language patterns:**
- "could", "might", "exploring whether", "open to shaping"
- "indicative", "draft", "candidate"
- Soft asks: "a short follow-up conversation" not "a signed MoU"
- Acknowledge BC's / audience's prerogative to shape the work
- Avoid: "we will deliver", "guaranteed", "first globally", "definitive"

**Headline pattern:** Statements of possibility. "A readiness layer that **could** sit alongside..."

## commit — confident proposal / signed-up audience

**Use when:** the relationship is established and the audience expects clear commitments.

**Language patterns:**
- "delivers", "drives", "lifts", "we will"
- Concrete metrics, dated milestones
- Strong asks: "sign the SoW", "approve the scope", "release the budget"
- Avoid: hedging language, "could", "might"

**Headline pattern:** Statements of action. "A readiness layer that **lifts** first-attempt pass rates by..."

## formal — board / regulator / committee

**Use when:** the audience is a formal body that requires evidence-led, restrained language.

**Language patterns:**
- Third-person where possible ("the proposal recommends" vs "we recommend")
- Cite sources for every claim
- No exclamation, no italics for emphasis, no rhetorical flourishes
- Use full names and titles
- Conservative numbers; range estimates over point estimates

**Headline pattern:** Descriptive, not persuasive. "Proposal: a 3–6 month pilot of a 4Cs readiness diagnostic across two product lines."

## Universal rules (apply to all tones)

- Never include speaker asides on the slide. They go in the speaker notes section of the markdown.
- Action titles only — every slide title states a conclusion or argument, not just a topic.
- Numbers must come from the user's context, public sources cited in the deck, or be flagged as illustrative/directional.
- Never invent quotes, names, or events.
