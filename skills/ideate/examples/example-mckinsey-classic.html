The platform in one slide

mySATHI is the readiness diagnostic that sits before the exam, the training programme and the application.

What it does
  • 4Cs assessment — Critical Thinking, Creativity, Collaboration, Communication
  • Computer-adaptive engine grounded in Item Response Theory (IRT)
  • Continuous tracking — longitudinal student progression, not point-in-time scores
  • NEP 2020 aligned — capability-based, not marks-centric
  • Proctored delivery across a pan-India centre network
What it produces
  • Readiness Index — a single, scaled, defensible number per student
  • 4C sub-scores with growth trajectories
  • Cohort dashboards for partners — pass-rate predictors, gap maps, segment reports
  • Selection / shortlisting filters for scholarship and mobility programmes
  • Diagnostic-to-intervention linkages for training programmes

The construct

The 4Cs are the capabilities UK universities and global employers actually select on.

Each is operationalised as a measurable, IRT-calibrated construct — not a self-report scale.

01
Critical Thinking
Reasoning under ambiguity, evaluating evidence, structured problem decomposition — relevant to case-based UK pedagogy and reasoning-heavy items in CFA / ACCA.
02
Creativity
Divergent generation, novel framing, applied originality — relevant to design / strategy programmes and to entrepreneurial readiness.
03
Collaboration
Working under joint ownership, conflict resolution, role flexibility — particularly relevant in the group-driven format of many UK Master's programmes.
04
Communication
Audience-aware written and verbal output, structured argumentation — designed to complement Cambridge English by adding a capability lens alongside proficiency.

Use case A — UK exam delivery

A readiness screen that runs before CFA, ACCA, Cambridge English — to lift pass rates and protect exam-board reputation.

How it could work
  1. 01.   Candidate registers for an upcoming exam window with BC.
  2. 02.   mySATHI screen offered alongside the registration flow — proctored, ~45 minutes.
  3. 03.   Readiness Index returned to BC and the candidate.
  4. 04.   Below-threshold candidates routed to BC's English / skills training — or to deferred exam slots.
  5. 05.   Above-threshold candidates progress with confidence.
Why BC wins
  • Stronger candidate funnel — supports first-attempt pass rates and exam-board confidence in India
  • Potential value-add — readiness screen could be offered alongside registration as an optional layer
  • Natural cross-sell into training — diagnostic flags can route candidates into BC English / skills programmes
  • Joint data signal — opportunity to build longitudinal exam-readiness benchmarks over time
  • Differentiated positioning — among the early exam delivery partnerships to layer in capability-based readiness

Use case B — English & professional skills training

Diagnostic-first delivery — so corporate and university clients buy outcomes, not seat-time.

The shift

Today, BC's training programmes typically run a common curriculum across mixed-ability cohorts, with outcomes reported at end-of-programme attainment level.

With mySATHI: a cohort begins with a 4Cs diagnostic. Intake data could inform personalisation; the exit snapshot quantifies capability uplift — not just hours delivered.

What BC ships to the client
  • Cohort intake report — 4C distribution, gap map, recommended track allocation
  • Personalisation hooks — which modules to emphasise per learner segment
  • Mid-programme checkpoint — adaptive course-correct
  • Exit certification — capability uplift, scaled and defensible
  • Year-on-year benchmarking — for repeat clients, longitudinal value

The same engine could support English-specific tracks (paired with Cambridge English) and broader professional skills programmes for corporates and universities.
